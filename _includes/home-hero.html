My research lies at the intersection of Public Economics and Political Economy, with a focus on gender-relevant topics. I am particularly interested in studying how social norms interact with the design and implementation of government policy.

My empirical work draws upon machine learning algorithms and social media data to quantify two main effects: (1) the extent to which social norms affect how legislators design gender-related policies, and (2) how these norms attenuate (or exacerbate) their effects on household behavior.
